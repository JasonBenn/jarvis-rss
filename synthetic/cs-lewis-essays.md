# CS Lewis - Great Essays

A curated collection of CS Lewis's most essential essays, released monthly.

## About

Clive Staples Lewis (1898-1963) was a British writer and theologian,
best known for The Chronicles of Narnia and his Christian apologetics.
His essays combine rigorous logic with accessible prose and timeless insights
into human nature, morality, and meaning.

## Curation Notes

This collection focuses on his standalone essays rather than excerpts from
longer works. Priority given to pieces that are:
- Accessible to modern readers
- Available freely online
- Representative of his range (apologetics, literary criticism, ethics)

## Items

### The Abolition of Man
- url: https://archive.org/details/TheAbolitionOfMan/mode/2up
- published: 2026-02-01
- description: Lewis's critique of moral relativism and the making of "men without chests." A prophetic warning about education that abandons objective value.
- notes: His most important philosophical work. Start here.

### Meditation in a Toolshed
- url: https://www.lewissociety.org/meditation-in-a-toolshed/
- published: 2026-03-01
- description: On the difference between looking AT and looking ALONG - a crucial distinction for understanding experience, science, and meaning.
- notes: Short, accessible, and profound. Good second essay.

### The Inner Ring
- url: https://www.lewissociety.org/innerring/
- published: 2026-04-01
- description: A Memorial Lecture on the human desire to be "in" and how this desire corrupts. Essential reading on social dynamics and integrity.
- notes: Given as a Memorial Lecture at King's College, 1944.

### Learning in War-Time
- url: https://www.lewissociety.org/learning-in-wartime/
- published: 2026-05-01
- description: A sermon on why pursuing knowledge matters even amid crisis. Relevant whenever the world feels like it's falling apart.
- notes: Preached at Oxford, Autumn 1939.

### The Weight of Glory
- url: https://www.wheelersburg.net/Downloads/Lewis%20Glory.pdf
- published: 2026-06-01
- description: Perhaps his most beautiful sermon - on desire, heaven, and the glory hidden in every person you meet.
- notes: Preached at Oxford, June 1942. His personal favorite of his sermons.

### On the Reading of Old Books
- url: https://www.lewissociety.org/on-the-reading-of-old-books/
- published: 2026-07-01
- description: Why we should read old books alongside new ones - the only way to escape the blind spots of our own era.
- notes: Originally the introduction to Athanasius's "On the Incarnation."

### Bulverism
- url: https://www.lewissociety.org/bulverism/
- published: 2026-08-01
- description: On the modern habit of explaining away an opponent's position rather than refuting it. "You say that because you're a..."
- notes: More relevant than ever in the age of "you only believe X because of your Y."

### The Humanitarian Theory of Punishment
- url: https://www.lewissociety.org/humanitarian-theory/
- published: 2026-09-01
- description: A defense of retributive justice against "therapeutic" punishment. Counterintuitive but compelling.
- notes: Published in 20th Century: An Australian Quarterly Review, 1949.

### Transposition
- url: https://www.lewissociety.org/transposition/
- published: 2026-10-01
- description: How higher realities are expressed through lower media - the body/soul relationship, art, religious experience.
- notes: A Whitsunday sermon, dense but rewarding.

### Is Theology Poetry?
- url: https://www.lewissociety.org/is-theology-poetry/
- published: 2026-11-01
- description: Is Christian belief just appealing mythology? Lewis's response to the charge that faith is wish-fulfillment.
- notes: Read to the Oxford Socratic Club, 1944.

### The Seeing Eye
- url: https://www.lewissociety.org/the-seeing-eye/
- published: 2026-12-01
- description: On space exploration, other worlds, and what we might find (or fail to find) out there.
- notes: Originally titled "Onward, Christian Spacemen" - written for a science magazine.

### Membership
- url: https://www.lewissociety.org/membership/
- published: 2027-01-01
- description: On the difference between true community (the Body) and mere collectivism. Individuals vs. persons.
- notes: Good pairing with "The Inner Ring."
