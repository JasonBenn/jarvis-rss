# On True Friendship

A curated reading list exploring the nature of true friendship across
philosophy, literature, and theology.

## About

What makes a friendship real? How does it differ from mere acquaintance,
professional networking, or romantic love? This collection gathers the
essential writings on friendship from Aristotle to the present.

## Curation Notes

Ordered roughly chronologically to show how thinking about friendship
has evolved. Emphasis on pieces that are:
- Substantive (not listicles or self-help)
- Available online
- From diverse traditions and eras

## Items

### Aristotle - Nicomachean Ethics, Books VIII-IX
- url: https://classics.mit.edu/Aristotle/nicomachaen.8.viii.html
- published: 2026-02-01
- description: The foundation of Western thought on friendship. Aristotle distinguishes friendships of utility, pleasure, and virtue - only the last being true friendship.
- notes: Dense but essential. The source of "a friend is another self."

### Cicero - De Amicitia (On Friendship)
- url: https://www.gutenberg.org/files/2808/2808-h/2808-h.htm
- published: 2026-02-15
- description: A Roman statesman's dialogue on friendship, written after the death of his friend Scipio. Warm, practical, and moving.
- notes: More accessible than Aristotle, deeply felt.

### Michel de Montaigne - Of Friendship
- url: https://www.gutenberg.org/files/3600/3600-h/3600-h.htm#link2HCH0027
- published: 2026-03-01
- description: Montaigne's tribute to his friend Etienne de La Boetie - "If you press me to say why I loved him, I feel it can only be expressed by replying - Because it was him; because it was me."
- notes: The most personal essay in the collection.

### Ralph Waldo Emerson - Friendship
- url: https://emersoncentral.com/texts/essays-first-series/friendship/
- published: 2026-03-15
- description: The transcendentalist take on friendship - high-minded, idealistic, somewhat austere. "A friend is a person with whom I may be sincere."
- notes: Beautiful language, though his vision of friendship may feel demanding.

### CS Lewis - Friendship (from The Four Loves)
- url: https://archive.org/details/TheFourLoves/mode/2up
- published: 2026-04-01
- description: Lewis defends friendship against those who see it as suspect or dispensable. "Friendship is unnecessary, like philosophy, like art... It has no survival value; rather it is one of those things which give value to survival."
- notes: The chapter on Friendship from The Four Loves. Perhaps the best modern treatment.

### Simone Weil - Friendship
- url: https://archive.org/details/waitingforgod0000weil/page/206/mode/2up
- published: 2026-04-15
- description: From "Waiting for God" - Weil's austere but luminous reflections on friendship as a form of implicit love of God.
- notes: Mystical, challenging, beautiful.

### Dietrich Bonhoeffer - Life Together (excerpts)
- url: https://archive.org/details/lifetogether00bonh
- published: 2026-05-01
- description: Written from the underground seminary at Finkenwalde, Bonhoeffer reflects on Christian community and what binds people together in genuine fellowship.
- notes: From a man who knew the cost of true friendship under tyranny.

### Aelred of Rievaulx - Spiritual Friendship
- url: https://archive.org/details/spiritualfriends00aelr
- published: 2026-05-15
- description: A 12th-century Cistercian monk's treatise on friendship, synthesizing Cicero with Christianity. "God is friendship."
- notes: The medieval masterwork on friendship. Surprisingly moving.
