# GK Chesterton - Essays

A curated collection of GK Chesterton's most brilliant essays, released monthly.

## About

Gilbert Keith Chesterton (1874-1936) was an English writer, philosopher, and
Christian apologist known for his wit, paradoxes, and defense of common sense
against fashionable thinking. His essays are characterized by their playful
style, surprising inversions, and deep wisdom hidden in apparent jokes.

## Curation Notes

Chesterton wrote prolifically - thousands of essays across dozens of publications.
This selection focuses on:
- Standalone essays that work without context
- Pieces showcasing his distinctive paradoxical style
- Essays that remain surprisingly relevant to modern debates

## Items

### A Defence of Nonsense
- url: https://www.gutenberg.org/files/12245/12245-h/12245-h.htm#A_DEFENCE_OF_NONSENSE
- published: 2026-02-01
- description: Chesterton's spirited defense of nonsense literature as a rebellion against the tyranny of rationalism. "Nonsense is a vacation from reason."
- notes: From "The Defendant" (1901). Sets the tone for his entire career.

### On Running After One's Hat
- url: https://www.gutenberg.org/files/11505/11505-h/11505-h.htm
- published: 2026-03-01
- description: On finding adventure in inconvenience. Chesterton transforms chasing a hat in the wind into a meditation on seeing life as romance rather than drudgery.
- notes: From "All Things Considered" (1908). Pure Chesterton.

### A Piece of Chalk
- url: https://www.gutenberg.org/files/11505/11505-h/11505-h.htm
- published: 2026-04-01
- description: A simple trip to draw on the downs becomes a meditation on whiteness, virtue, and the startling philosophy hidden in a piece of chalk.
- notes: One of his most anthologized essays. Deceptively simple.

### The Fallacy of Success
- url: https://www.gutenberg.org/files/11505/11505-h/11505-h.htm
- published: 2026-05-01
- description: A devastating critique of self-help literature and the worship of "success" divorced from any definition of what success means.
- notes: Written in 1908 but reads like it was written yesterday about LinkedIn.

### On Lying in Bed
- url: https://www.gutenberg.org/files/8092/8092-h/8092-h.htm
- published: 2026-06-01
- description: Chesterton defends the art of staying in bed, argues for painting the ceiling, and somehow ends up making a profound point about modern life.
- notes: From "Tremendous Trifles" (1909).

### The Twelve Men
- url: https://www.gutenberg.org/files/8092/8092-h/8092-h.htm
- published: 2026-07-01
- description: A defense of the jury system as the most radical institution in democracy - putting absolute power in the hands of twelve ordinary citizens.
- notes: A stirring argument for common sense over expertise.

### The Secret of Father Brown
- url: https://www.gutenberg.org/files/223/223-h/223-h.htm
- published: 2026-08-01
- description: Father Brown explains his detective method: not deduction, but imagination - imagining himself as the criminal to understand the crime.
- notes: From the Father Brown stories. Philosophy disguised as fiction.

### What's Wrong with the World (Introduction)
- url: https://www.gutenberg.org/files/1717/1717-h/1717-h.htm
- published: 2026-09-01
- description: Chesterton's famous answer to "What's wrong with the world?" - "I am." But the full introduction is a brilliant critique of reform movements.
- notes: Just the introduction, not the whole book. Sets up his critique of progressivism.

### The Ethics of Elfland
- url: https://www.gutenberg.org/files/470/470-h/470-h.htm#link2H_4_0005
- published: 2026-10-01
- description: From Orthodoxy - Chesterton's philosophical autobiography and perhaps his greatest work. This chapter on fairy tales and science is the heart of the book.
- notes: Chapter 4 of Orthodoxy. His most important philosophical writing.

### The Maniac
- url: https://www.gutenberg.org/files/470/470-h/470-h.htm#link2H_4_0002
- published: 2026-11-01
- description: Chesterton's argument that the problem with the madman is not that he lacks reason, but that he has nothing but reason. A critique of pure rationalism.
- notes: Chapter 2 of Orthodoxy. Pairs well with "The Ethics of Elfland."

### The Flag of the World
- url: https://www.gutenberg.org/files/470/470-h/470-h.htm#link2H_4_0006
- published: 2026-12-01
- description: On the paradox of loving the world enough to want to change it - the optimist and pessimist both fail; the lover succeeds.
- notes: Chapter 5 of Orthodoxy. The emotional climax of the book.
